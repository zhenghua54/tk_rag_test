When the Child-Protection Door Lock system is engaged, the door can be opened only by using the outside door handle, even though the inside door lock is in the unlocked position.  After disengaging the Child-Protection Door Lock system, always test the door from the inside to make certain it is in the unlocked position.  After engaging the Child-Protection Door Lock system, always test the door from the inside to make certain it is in the locked position.  For emergency exit with the system engaged, move the lock lever rearward (located on the door trim panel), lower the window and open the door with the outside door handle.  Avoid trapping anyone in a vehicle in a collision.  Remember that the rear doors can only be opened from the outside when the Child-Protection Door Lock system is engaged (locked).
