Mikhail Vasilyevich Lomonosov ( ; Russian: Михаи́л (Михáйло) Васи́льевич Ломоно́сов ; ] ; November 19 [O.S. November 8] 1711 – April 15 [O.S. April 4] 1765 ) was a Russian polymath, scientist and writer, who made important contributions to literature, education, and science. Among his discoveries were the atmosphere of Venus and the Law of Mass Conservation in chemical reactions. His spheres of science were natural science, chemistry, physics, mineralogy, history, art, philology, optical devices and others. Lomonosov was also a poet and influenced the formation of the modern Russian literary language.
