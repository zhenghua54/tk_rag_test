Joel David Coen (born November 29, 1954) and Ethan Jesse Coen (born September 21, 1957), collectively referred to as the Coen brothers, are American filmmakers. Their films span many genres and styles, which they frequently subvert or parody. Their best-reviewed works include ""Miller's Crossing" (1990), "Fargo" (1996), "The Big Lebowski" (1998), "No Country for Old Men" (2007), "A Serious Man" (2009), "True Grit" (2010), and "Inside Llewyn Davis" (2013).
