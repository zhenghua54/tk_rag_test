The Inn (Latin: "Aenus" ; Romansh: "En" ) is a river in Switzerland, Austria and Germany. It is a right tributary of the Danube and is 517 km long. The highest point of its drainage basin is the summit of Piz Bernina, at 4049 m . The Engadine, the valley of the En, is the only Swiss valley from which its waters ends in the Black Sea (via the Danube).
