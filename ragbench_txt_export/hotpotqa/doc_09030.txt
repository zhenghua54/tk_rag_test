Santería, also known as Regla de Ochá, La Regla de Ifá, or Lucumi, is an Afro-American religion of Caribbean origin that developed in the Spanish Empire among West African descendants. "Santeria" is a Spanish word that means the "worship of saints". Santería is influenced by and syncretized with Roman Catholicism. Its sacred language, a variety of Yoruba, is the Lucumí language.
