Dasht-e Kavir (Persian: دشت كوير‎ ‎ , 'Low Plains' in classical Persian, from "khwar" (low), and "dasht" (plain), flatland), also known as Kavir-e Namak ( 'salty lowlands') and the Great Salt Desert, is a large desert lying in the middle of the Iranian plateau. It is about 800 km by 320 km with a total surface area of about 77600 sqkm , making it the Earth's 26th largest desert. The area of this desert stretches from the Alborz mountain range in the north-west to the Dasht-e Lut in the south-east and is partitioned between the Iranian provinces of Khorasan, Semnan, Tehran, Isfahan and Yazd
