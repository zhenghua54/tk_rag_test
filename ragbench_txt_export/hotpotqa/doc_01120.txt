Tashkyopryu Mosque (Bulgarian: Ташкьопрю джамия "Tashkyopryu Dzhamiya", Turkish: "Taşköprü Camii") is a mosque in Plovdiv, Bulgaria , built by Ottoman Turks in 16th century during their 500-year rule in today's Bulgaria. It is currently the third mosque in Plovdiv which is in good condition after Dzhumaya Mosque and Imaret Mosque. It is currently inactive despite efforts and demands, mainly by Turks of Bulgaria, to give active status for it.
