Jaime Fernández Reyes (December 6, 1937 – April 15, 2005) was a Mexican actor. Over his career, he won 3 Silver Ariel awards - the Mexican equivalent of the Oscar - including one for what is arguably his best-known role, playing Friday in Luis Buñuel's "Robinson Crusoe". He appeared in over 200 films and served as the General Secretary of the Mexican actors' union for 11 years. His older brother was actor/director Emilio Fernández.
