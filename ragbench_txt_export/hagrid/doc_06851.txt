Bluebonnet is a name given to any number of blue-flowered species of the genus "Lupinus" predominantly found in southwestern United States and is collectively the state flower of Texas. The shape of the petals on the flower resembles the bonnet worn by pioneer women to shield them from the sun. So, in 1971, the Texas Legislature made any similar species of "Lupinus" that could be found in Texas the state flower.
