Each province's certifying body is responsible for determining the standards by which Engineering Technologists are certified. However, most jurisdictions do have some commonalities:Certified Engineering Technologist abbreviated "C.E.T." Registered Engineering Technologist abbreviated "R.E.T." and Applied Science Technologist", abbreviated "A.Sc.T." are voluntary certifications and are not a provincial regulatory requirement or a statutory required license, there is no legal requirement for membership. Certification is managed individually by each province. In Alberta Professional Technologists abbreviated "P.Tech" are required to have a limited license to practice professional engineering within a defined scope.
