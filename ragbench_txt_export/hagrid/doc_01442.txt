In 1991, Sid Meier released "Civilization" and popularized the level of detail that has become common in the genre. "Sid Meier's Civilization" was influenced by board games such as "Risk" and the Avalon Hill board game also called "Civilization". A notable similarity between the "Civilization" computer game and board game is the importance of diplomacy and technological advancement. Sid Meier's "Civilization" was also influenced by personal computer games such as the city management game "SimCity" and the wargame "Empire". "Civilization" became widely successful and influenced many 4X games to come; "Computer Gaming World" compared its importance to computer gaming to that of the wheel.
