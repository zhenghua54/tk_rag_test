Rock served as President of the Student Federation of the University of Ottawa for the 1969-70 year. As President, he met John Lennon in June 1969 and drove him around Ottawa while he was hosting him for a "peace conference" he was holding. He had invited Lennon to Ottawa after his famous "bed-in" in Montreal. He tried to introduce Lennon to Pierre Trudeau, but the prime minister was not at home when they dropped by. He had also previously served as an executive for the Arts students association and briefly as SFUO External Commissioner.
