The złoty (pronounced ; sign: zł; code: PLN), which is the masculine form of the Polish adjective 'golden', is the currency of Poland. The modern złoty is subdivided into 100 groszy (singular: "grosz"; alternative plural form: "grosze"). The recognised English form of the word is "zloty", plural zloty or zlote. The currency sign, zł, is composed of the Polish lower-case letters "z" and "ł" (Unicode: & ).
