When Mexico gained independence in 1821, the casta system was eliminated as a legal structure, but racial divides remained.  White Mexican argued about what the solution was to the "Indian Problem", that is indigenous who continued to live in communities and were not integrated politically or socially as citizens of the new republic. The Mexican constitution of 1824 has several articles pertaining to indigenous peoples.
