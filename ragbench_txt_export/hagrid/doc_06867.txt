Dire Dawa (, ,  "Place of Remedy", ,  "where Dir hit his spear into the ground" or "The true Dir", ) is one of two chartered cities ("astedader akabibi") in Ethiopia (the other being the capital, Addis Ababa). It is divided administratively into two woredas, the city proper and the non-urban woreda of Gurgura. 
Dire Dawa lies in the eastern part of the nation, on the Dechatu River, at the foot of a ring of cliffs that has been described as "somewhat like a cluster of tea-leaves in the bottom of a slop-basin." The western outskirts of the city lie on the Gorro River, a tributary of the Dechatu River. At a latitude and longitude of , Dire Dawa is the second largest city in Ethiopia.
