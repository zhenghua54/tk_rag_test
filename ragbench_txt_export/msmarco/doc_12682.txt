They occupied the desert regions of eastern Syria and present day Jordan, on the edge of the Levant. Also like their cousins, the Kedarites claimed an Israelite ancestor figure, this time being Kedar, second son of Ismael, who was himself one of the sons of Abraham. Kingdom of Kedar (Qedar / Qidri). c.870 - 610 BC. Subdued by the resurgent Assyrian empire in the ninth century BC, The Kedarite kingdom survived as a vassal state, occasionally rebellious, but mostly compliant.
