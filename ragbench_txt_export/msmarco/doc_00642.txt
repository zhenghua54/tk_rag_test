Click for detailed map. Sikhism's holiest place of worship is the Golden Temple, or Harmandir, in the city of Amritsar. Located in the state of Punjab, the city, established to be a place of pilgrimage, derives its name from the sacred tank, or pool, called the Amrita Saras (pool of nectar), where the temple was built. The Golden Temple reflecting in the Amrita Saras. Golden Temple is the name of Amritsar's hallowed Sikh shrine and the religious complex where it's located. The shimmering two-story temple sits atop a square platform in the center of a man-made tank, called the Amrita Saras (pool of nectar).
