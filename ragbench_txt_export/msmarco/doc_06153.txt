This graph displays the average monthly churn rate for top wireless carriers in the United States from the first quarter of 2013 to the second quarter of 2015. The average monthly churn rate of Verizon Wireless was at 1.26 percent in the fourth quarter of 2013. Churn rates of wireless carriers-additional information. The average monthly churn rate of wireless carriers refers to the average percentage of subscribers that cease to use the company’s services per month. AT&T’s churn rate in the second quarter of 2014 stood at 1.47 percent, the second lowest of all the wireless carriers in the U.S. The Texas-based company’s churn rate has remained relatively stable in recent years, although it has risen slightly since it was at its lowest of 1.31 percent in 2010.
