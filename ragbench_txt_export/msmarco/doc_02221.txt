The name Basil (/ˈbaezəl/ ; ˈbæzəl, royal) kingly comes from the Male greek Name (Vassilios: GREEK, βασίλειος female Version), bασιλική which first appeared during The hellenistic. period It was brought to England by the Crusaders, having been common in the eastern Mediterranean. It is more often used in Britain and Europe than in the US and is also the name of a common herb. In Arabic, Bas(s)el (باسل, bāsil) is an indirect Quranic name for boys that means brave, fearless, intrepid.
