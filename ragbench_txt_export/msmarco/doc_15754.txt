Ethnic cleansing vs. genocide [edit]. The crimes committed during an ethnic cleansing are similar to those of genocides, but while genocide includes an intent at complete or partial destruction of the target group, ethnic cleansing may involve murder only to the point of mobilizing the target group out of the territory. At one end it is virtually indistinguishable from forced emigration and population exchange while at the other it merges with deportation and genocide.
