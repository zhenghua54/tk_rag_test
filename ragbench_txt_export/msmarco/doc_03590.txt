The Model T ran on (ethanol) alcohol, fuel or a combination of the two fuels. 1940's First U.S. fuel ethanol plant built. The U.S. Army built and operated an ethanol plant in Omaha, Nebraska, to produce fuel for the army and to provide ethanol for regional fuel blending. Automobiles that can run on E85 are usually referred to as Flex Fuel or Alternative Fuel Vehicles, (FFV and AFV). The vehicle's gas cap will always state when up to 85% ethanol (E85) is permitted. E85 fuel should never be used in a conventional gas-powered vehicle.
