The name adzuki is a transliteration of the native Japanese name. Japanese also has a Chinese loanword, shōzu (小豆) , which means small bean, its counterpart large bean (大豆, daizu) being the soybean. It is common to write 小豆 in kanji but pronounce it as azuki listen (help·info) , an example of ateji. Adzuki beans are also used to produce amanattō, and as a popular flavour of ice cream either paste, or whole bean such as the 'Cream & Red Bean' product produced by IMEI. On October 20, 2009, Pepsi Japan released an adzuki-flavored Pepsi product.
