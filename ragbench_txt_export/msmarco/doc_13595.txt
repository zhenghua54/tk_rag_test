If I am denied a pardon, do I have to wait an entire year from the hearing date to re-apply for a pardon? -Generally you must wait one year from the date of denial before you can reapply. The Board may at its discretion specify a longer waiting period. There are two types of pardons: An Expungement pardon is an erasure of the official criminal record. You may apply to the Board for an expungement 3 years after the date of the disposition of your most recent misdemeanor conviction and/or 5 years after the date of the disposition of your most recent felony conviction.
