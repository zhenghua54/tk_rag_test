The climate of Komodo is fairly hot and dry with a short rainy season that lasts from January to April. The island receives an average rainfall of about 800 to 1000 mm annually. The Komodo National Park receives the least amount of rain in Indonesia. It experiences a very short spell of rain in January. The effects of the long spell of dry weather are evident in the species of flora and fauna native to the region. Lesser Sunda Islands. In complete contrast to the climate of Komodo, the climate of the Lesser Sunda Islands is tropical like the rest of Indonesia. It has a distinct wet and dry season. The wet season generally lasts from November to March while the dry season lasts from April to October.
