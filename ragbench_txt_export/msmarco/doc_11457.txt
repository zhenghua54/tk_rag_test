Finnish (Suomi) is a member of the Finnic branch of the Uralic language family. It is not an Indo-European language. Among its closest living relatives are Karelian, Veps, Ingrian and Estonian, and the almost extinct Votic and Livonian. Finnish is spoken by 4.7 million people in the Republic of Finland. It is also spoken in Canada, Estonia, Norway, Russian Federation, Sweden, United States.
