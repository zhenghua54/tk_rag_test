Nothing beats homemade pasta—not even store-bought “fresh.” This is homemade pasta made the traditional way—by hand. A mixture of 50% Italian “00” flour and 50% Farina di Semola (pale yellow, finely ground, hard durum wheat flour for making pasta and some bread) works particularly well. 1 9. 2  Throw the pasta into a large saucepan of boiling, salted water. 3  You will need about 4 quarts water and 3 tablespoons of salt for every 13 to 18 ounces of fresh or dried pasta. 4  It is the large volume of water that will prevent the pasta from sticking together.
