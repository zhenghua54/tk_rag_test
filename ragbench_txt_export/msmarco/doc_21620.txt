Foie gras is a delicacy with a unique, refined taste. Foie gras is the liver of a goose or duck specially fattened through a feeding process for a specific period of time. Fattening: the English translation of Gavage, which comes from the French word ‘gave’, originating in the ancient region of Picardie. More duck foie gras is eaten than goose foie gras. It’s more rustic with a stronger taste. Its bold taste appeals to many gourmets and it now accounts for over 90% of French foie gras production. Goose foie gras is delicate and sweet on the palate. Some gourmets find it more subtle than duck
