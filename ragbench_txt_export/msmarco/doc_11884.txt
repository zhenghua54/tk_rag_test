Archaebacteria are a type of prokaryote, that is, a unicellular organism without a cell nucleus. They make up the kingdom Archae, one of the main kingdoms of life. These organisms are difficult to classify because they have similarities to both normal bacteria and the larger eukaryotes. Archaebacteria are unicellular organisms that lack a nucleus. A type of extremophile, archaebacteria use chemical tricks to survive in harsh environments, such as volcanic lakes.
