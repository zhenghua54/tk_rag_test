The Blue-faced parrot finch inhabits forests edges, cultivated areas and plantations. They are sometimes found in flocks of 15-30 birds feeding on seeds of grasses, fruits, figs and a variety of live foods including termites and other small insects. Description: Length: 4 to 4 inches (11.5 to 12 cm). The Blue-faced parrot finch is heavy built bird it has a vivid blue face mask. The upper parts of the male's body are rich green. The under parts extending from the throat downward is a much lighter green. The rump and upper tail coverts are red
