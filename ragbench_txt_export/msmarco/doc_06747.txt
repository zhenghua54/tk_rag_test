Background. Kerosene is an oil distillate commonly used as a fuel or solvent. It is a thin, clear liquid consisting of a mixture of hydrocarbons that boil between 302°F and 527°F (150°C and 275°C). While kerosene can be extracted from coal, oil shale, and wood, it is primarily derived from refined petroleum. KEROSENE. A flammable hydrocarbon oil, kerosene is a petroleum product primarily used for fuel. In 1854 Canadian geologist Abraham Gesner (1797 – 1864) discovered a process for distilling fuel from petroleum (initially, coal oil).
