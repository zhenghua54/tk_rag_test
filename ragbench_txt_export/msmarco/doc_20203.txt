SHARE SHARE TWEET EMAIL. Range of motion is the capacity for movement at a given joint in a specific direction. The anatomy of a joint dictates the normal limits of its range of motion. Health care professionals assess joint movement with two types of range-of-motion testing: active and passive. Therefore, the range of motion for a given joint is tested for each type of movement possible at the site. Flexion refers to the bending capacity of joints, such as your elbows, hips and knees.
