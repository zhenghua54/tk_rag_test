The thymus gland is an organ in the upper chest cavity that processes lymphocytes, a type of white blood cell that fights infections in the body. This organ is part of both the lymphatic system, which makes up a major part of the immune system, and the endocrine system, which includes all glands that produce hormones. The thymus gland is most important to children and young adults as it programs white blood cells to attack antigens. Different types of white blood cells programmed within the thymus gland. Chest pain may indicate a tumor of the thymus gland. The thymus is located just below the throat, behind the sternum.
