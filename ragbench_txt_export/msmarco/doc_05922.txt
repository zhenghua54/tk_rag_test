The two main coronary arteries are the left and right coronary arteries. The left coronary artery (LCA), which divides into the left anterior descending artery and the circumflex branch, supplies blood to the heart ventricles and left atrium. Additional arteries branch off the two main coronary arteries to supply the heart muscle with blood.
