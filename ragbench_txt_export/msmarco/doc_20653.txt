1 Corticosteroid pills (usually prednisone) can dramatically reduce the symptoms caused by a strong reaction to poison ivy, oak, or sumac. 2  Oral corticosteroids generally work better than other forms of these medicines for poison ivy, oak, or sumac. 3  And they are usually taken until the symptoms are gone. High-dose topical corticosteroids should not be confused with over-the-counter hydrocortisone creams, gels, or ointments, which may soothe itching in mild cases of poison ivy, oak, or sumac rash. These products are not recommended for severe rashes
