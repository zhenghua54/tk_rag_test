1 Celiac disease is thought to be an autoimmune disorder and may have a familial or genetic component. 2  The symptoms usually involve the digestive system and cause: 3  abdominal discomfort, 4  bloating, 5  nausea, and. 6  loose bowel movements. 7  However, there is a wide spectrum of symptoms that may occur. Signs and symptoms of celiac disease may include: 1  diarrhea, 2  bloating, 3  gas (flatulence, farting), 4  swollen ankles (edema), 5  anemia, 6  fatigue, 7  vitamin K deficiency, and. 8  excessive bruising and bleeding.
