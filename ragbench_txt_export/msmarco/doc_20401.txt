In their 1999 book, Diagnosing and Changing Organizational Culture, Kim Cameron and Robert Quinn listed four basic types of business cultures: the Hierarchy, the Market, the Adhocracy, and the Clan cultures -- with the clan culture being the most laid-back one on the list. Potential for Abuse. A clan culture is open, friendly and arguably more appreciative of its employees than any other. However, this comfortable culture is vulnerable to abuse if employees use its tolerance as an opportunity to relax rather than an opportunity to contribute.
