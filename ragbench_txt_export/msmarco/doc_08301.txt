Adjectives that are in the comparative form can be modified by intensifiers such as much, far and sometimes by adverbs of degree (examples 8). Some kinds of adjectives, notably participles, can be modified by a wide range of adverbs (examples 9 and 10). This is the most common use of adjectives, standing next to a noun in a noun phrase. In English, simple and complex adjectives almost always come before the noun. There are only a very small number of exceptions, For details on the ordering of adjectives within a noun group, see adjective order.
