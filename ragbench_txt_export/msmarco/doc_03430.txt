Noun. 1. lac-resinlike substance secreted by certain lac insects; used in e.g. varnishes and sealing wax. seal, sealing wax-fastener consisting of a resinous composition that is plastic when warm; used for sealing documents and parcels and letters. 1. (Elements & Compounds) a resinous substance secreted by certain lac insects, used in the manufacture of shellac. [C16: from Dutch lak or French laque, from Hindi lākh resin, ultimately from Sanskrit lākshā ]. (laek). læk
