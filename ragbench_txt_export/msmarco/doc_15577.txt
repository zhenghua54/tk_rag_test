Fetal cardiac activity can be seen by 6 menstrual weeks’ gestation, and the fetal brain can be seen by 8 weeks. The crown–rump length can be used accurately to assess gestational age within 4 days up to approximately 12 weeks. Implantation, or the process by which the embryo comes in contact with, adheres to, and penetrates the endometrium, is necessary prior to the diagnosis of pregnancy.
