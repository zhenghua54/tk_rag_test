Anti-Federalists believed also that the Federalists were representing the interest of the aristocratic elements in society at the expense of the ordinary people in rural society. This was important at a time when the overwhelming majority of the population lived outside cities and earned a living by working the land. This series of essays was probably the most widely known expression of anti-federalist views. The first letter ends: Anti-Federalists were concerned that the constitution did not equally divide power among the three branches of government.
