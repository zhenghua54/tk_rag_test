With all plants, start by removing dead, diseased or dying branches. . Prune shrub roses in mid-spring. Remove about a quarter of growth and any old branches causing congestion. Prune bush roses in mid-spring. These can be pruned quite hard, reducing plants by up to a half or to within 8cm (3in) of the previous year’s growth. Prune climbers in autumn as flowers fade. Cut side branches to leave two leaf buds and tie in new shoots.
