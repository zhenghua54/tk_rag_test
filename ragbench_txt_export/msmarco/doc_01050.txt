It is a major pest of national parks and nature preserves in the western United States. Appearance. Euphorbia esula is an erect, perennial, herbaceous plant that grows from 2-3.5 ft. (0.6-1.1 m) tall. The stem is smooth and bluish-green. The plant produces a milky sap if stem is broken or a leaf is removed. Foliage. Leaves are lance shaped, smooth and 1-4 in. (2.5-10.2 cm) long
