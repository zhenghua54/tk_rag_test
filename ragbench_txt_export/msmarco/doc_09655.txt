Parkinson's disease was first described in 1817 by James Parkinson, a British doctor who published a paper on what he called the shaking palsy.. Diagnosis Can Be Difficult. About 60,000 Americans are diagnosed with Parkinson's disease each year. However, it's difficult to know exactly how many have it because many people in the early stages of the disease think their symptoms are due to normal aging and do not seek help from a doctor.
