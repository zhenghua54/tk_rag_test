The diagnosis of tuberculous aetiology in pericardial effusions is important since the prognosis is excellent with specific treatment. The clinical features may not be distinctive and the diagnosis could be missed particularly with tamponade. With the spread of HIV infection the incidence has increased. Tuberculous pericardial effusion (TPE) is still common in African and Asian countries, 1, 2 but with migrant populations and the spread of AIDS 3 there are now more frequent reports from other parts of the world like Europe and the Western hemisphere. 4, 5 TPE usually presents as a slowly progressive febrile illness.
