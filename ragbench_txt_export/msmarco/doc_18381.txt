Multiply total gallons by 95,475 to figure total BTUs of usage. To figure the total propane for your home, multiply total gallons of propane for the year by 95,475. The result is the total BTUs your family consumes in a year. Example: 800 gallons * 95,475 = 76,380,000 BTUs. According to Home Energy: Advancing Home Performance, 39.4 percent of homes using propane heating use between 5 and 10 British Thermal Units (BTU) per square foot. Only 12.2 percent use under 5 BTUs
