If a spiculated mass is the cause of breast cancer, it should be discovered by performing a mammogram. In cases of lung cancer, a CT scan of the chest will be able to detect a spiculated mass. Some studies have shown that a spiculated mass typically has at least 30 percent more progesterone receptors. A spiculated mass will be on the periphery of a breast in instances of breast cancer. Radiotherapy, such as with a linear accelerator, might be used to help treat a spiculated mass.
