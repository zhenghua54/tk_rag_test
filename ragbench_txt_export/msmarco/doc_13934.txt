C 6 H 5 NO 2, very poisonous, flammable, pale yellow, liquid aromatic compound with an odor like that of bitter almonds. It is sometimes called oil of mirbane or nitrobenzol. Nitrobenzene melts at 5.85&degC;, boils at 210.9&degC;, is only slightly soluble in water, but is very soluble in ethanol, ether, and benzene.
