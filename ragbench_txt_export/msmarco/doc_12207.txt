Hepatitis B is a serious disease caused by the hepatitis B virus. The virus can enter the bloodstream, attack the liver, and cause serious damage. When babies get infected, the virus usually remains in the body for a lifetime (this is called chronic hepatitis B). A baby who gets infected with the hepatitis B virus during the first five years of life has a 15% to 25% risk for pre­mature death from liver disease, including liver failure or liver cancer.
