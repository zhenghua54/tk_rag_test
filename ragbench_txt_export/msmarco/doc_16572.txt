Another cue you are experiencing pain and not muscle soreness is if you begin to feel symptoms in a part of your body not being exercised. For example, if your low back experiences pain while performing an abdominal exercise. This could be caused by the body’s normal response to build muscle (soreness) or due to more serious injury (pain). Many people have a difficult time distinguishing between soreness and pain.
