The term sonnet is derived from the Italian word sonetto (from Old Provencal provençal sonet a little, poem from son, song From latin sonus a). Sound by the thirteenth century it signified a poem of fourteen lines that follows a strict rhyme scheme and specific. structure He wrote almost 250 sonnets. Other Italian poets of the time, including Dante Alighieri (1265–1321) and Guido Cavalcanti (c. 1250–1300), wrote sonnets, but the most famous early sonneteer was Petrarca (known in English as Petrarch).
