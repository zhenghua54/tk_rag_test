Oil and natural gas prices, combined with full capacity world wide in the offshore rig market -- and competition for these fleets -- is hurting the back side of international oil companies, with drilling and production costs more than doubling in recent years. Expect $600,000 day rates to become more common. Last month, Brazilian Petrobras awarded Norway's SeaDrill with contracts for three deepwater rigs worth up to $4.1 billion, with day rates in excess of $600,000.
