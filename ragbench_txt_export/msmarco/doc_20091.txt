Misdemeanor Crimes. A misdemeanor is considered a less serious crime. Most states and the federal government classify a crime that’s punishable by less than one year in prison as a misdemeanor. Some states consider a misdemeanor as any crime that’s punishable only by fine or a small length of time in jail. If a misdemeanor is considered a very minor offense, such as jaywalking, the crime may be classified as a petty offense. There’s a wide variety of crimes that are considered misdemeanors in most states. Some common misdemeanors include: 1  Public intoxication. 2  Trespassing. 3  Speeding. 4  Prostitution. 5  Vandalism
