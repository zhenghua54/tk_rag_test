Iron is one of the minerals in the human body. It is one of the components of hemoglobin, the substance in red blood cells that helps blood carry oxygen throughout the body. If you do not have enough iron, your body cannot make hemoglobin, and you may develop anemia. 1 blood loss (caused by ulcers, some cancers, and other conditions; and, in women, during monthly periods); 2  a diet that doesn't have enough iron in it; 3  taking medications that interfere with the ability of the body to absorb iron; 4  an increase in the body's need for iron (for instance, in women during pregnancy).
