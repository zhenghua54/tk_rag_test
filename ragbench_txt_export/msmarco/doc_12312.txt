Application to Biotechnology. In the Biotech industry, Citrobacter freundii produces many important enzymes. The first enzymes it produces is phosphatase. Phosphatase activity of C. freundii has been postulated to be involved in lead accumulation, which can have play an important role in the Biotech industry. Ecology. Citrobacter freundii are commonly found in the environment, mainly in soil, water, and sewages. They are an indicator of potential contamination of water. They are also found on different organs of diseased animals, including mammals, birds, reptiles, and amphibians [1].
