If the Japanese Knotweed is sparsely distributed, spot-treat or use 2, 4-D amine, which is specific to broadleaved plants and will not harm the grasses. Japanese Knotweed. Japanese Knotweed was introduced in the UK in the 1850's as an ornamental plant and as a fodder plant. It is a very invasive weed though not harmful if consumed. It is generally found along rivers and streams, road verges, in gardens and on waste ground.
