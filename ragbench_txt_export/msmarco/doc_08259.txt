Plumeria rubra is the type most often grown, according to the Arizona Plumeria Society, and produces pink or red flowers. Suitable for U.S. Department of Agriculture plant hardiness zones 10 through 12, this plumeria grows 1 to 2 feet per year to a mature height of about 25 feet. Growing to about 30 feet tall, plumeria's round canopy spreads as wide as the tree is tall. Depending on species and cultivar, plumeria foliage may be as much as 12 inches long and the trees may be deciduous or evergreen.
