1 Scalp (tinea capitis): Ringworm on the scalp usually looks like a scaly, itchy, red, circular bald spot. 2  The bald spot can grow in size and multiple spots might develop if the infection spreads. 3  Ringworm on the scalp is more common in children than it is in adults. Ringworm can affect skin on almost any part of the body as well as fingernails and toenails. The symptoms of ringworm often depend on which part of the body is infected, but they generally include: 1  Itchy skin. 2  Ring-shaped rash. 3  Red, scaly, cracked skin. 4  Hair loss.
