Aftereffects. From King's perspective the Albany Movement was a failure, but African Americans in Albany disagreed. King's failure did not mean that the movement failed. SNCC field secretary Charles Sherrod remarked, Now I can't help how Dr. King might have felt, . . . but as far as we were concerned, things moved on. It was the first mass movement in the modern civil rights era to have as its goal the desegregation of an entire community, and it resulted in the jailing of more than 1,000 African Americans in Albany and surrounding rural counties.
