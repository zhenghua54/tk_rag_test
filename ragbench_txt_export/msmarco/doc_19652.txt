Incidentally, pheochromocytomas are called the 10% tumor because they are associated with a 10% risk of malignancy, because 10% of the tumors are bilateral, and because 10% of the tumors are extra-adrenal. Early detection may reduce the risk of metastasis. Usually, tumors are larger than 3 cm when seen.
