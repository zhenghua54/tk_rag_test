Vinson Massif, at 78°35'S, 85°25'W is 21km (13 miles) long and 13km (8 miles) wide, is the highest peak in Antarctica. It lies on the southern part of the main ridge of the Sentinel Range. It was named for Carl G. Vinson, a Georgia congressman and a major force in 20th century U.S. Antarctic exploration. 1 Scale the top of the bottom of the world - the highest mountain in Antarctica and one of the most remote mountains on earth. 2  Fly across the Drake Passage and over the sprawling icy interior of Antarctica to reach Vinson Base Camp.
