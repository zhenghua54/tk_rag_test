Antiphospholipid Antibodies. Antiphospholipid antibodies are antibodies directed against phosphorus-fat components of your cell membranes called phospholipids, certain blood proteins that bind with phospholipids, and the complexes formed when proteins and phospholipids bind. Antiphospholipid Antibody Syndrome (APS). Individuals who experience complications from antiphospholipid antibodies are diagnosed with Antiphospholipid Antibody Syndrome (APS). This condition can occur both in people with lupus and those without lupus. Fifty percent of people with lupus have APS.
