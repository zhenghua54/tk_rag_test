Salary. The paycheck is good. Financial analysts earned a median of $78,380 in 2013, with the lowest-paid making $48,100 and the highest-paid making $152,420. The best-paid in the profession work in the metropolitan areas of Bridgeport, Connecticut; New York City; and San Francisco. Many work for financial companies, including those in the financial services and insurance industries. For their influence and sizeable paychecks, financial analysts pay the price by working long hours: One in three put in between 50 and 70 hours a week.
