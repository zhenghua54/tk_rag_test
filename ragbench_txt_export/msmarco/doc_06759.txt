Domestic rabbit-, rodent-and hedgehog-related diseases of concern in Ohio include: 1  Animal bites: rabbits, rodents and pocket pets. 2  Dermatophytosis (ringworm). 3  Leptospirosis. 4  Lymphocytic choriomeningitis virus (LCMV). 5  Monkeypox. 6  Rat-bite fever. 7  Salmonellosis. T. mentagrophytes causes scaly white lesions around the head of mice and rabbits, although some animals may be inapparent carriers without signs of disease. People can develop dermatophytosis after contact with infected rodents or rabbits.
