Etiology, Epidemiology, and Transmission. The causal agent, Akabane virus, is a member of the Simbu serogroup of the family Bunyaviridae. It is spread by biting midges (Culicoides spp) in Australia, Japan, and Kenya. Akabane virus is common in many tropical and subtropical areas between ∼35°N and 35°S.
