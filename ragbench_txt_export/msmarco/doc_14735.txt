Desert rose is a slow grower with maximum observed heights of about 6 feet in South Florida but usually much smaller. Sap flows from broken stem of A. obesum Sap flows from severed leaf A. obesum This plant is approximately 35 years old and measures 6 feet high and 10 feet wide, Fort Myers, early April. Avoid planting desert rose in areas where automatic sprinklers are located. Fertilization and Irrigation: The plant is a slow grower if not generously watered and fertilized. With-out fertilization the plant may grow as little as 6 feet in 15 year. The plant has its fastest growth in the first 3 to 5 years.
