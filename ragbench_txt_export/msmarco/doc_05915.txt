General Information on the Geoffroy's Spider Monkey. Geoffroy's spider monkeys, some of the largest monkeys found in Central America, weigh about 20 pounds. They're the third smartest primate; chimpanzees and orangutans are the smartest. Geoffroy's spider monkeys are extremely vocal, and each individual has a unique voice recognized by other family members. Geoffroy's spider monkey, sometimes called the black-handed spider monkey, is long and slender, and has long spidery arms. His fur color varies from light brown or reddish to black, and his hairless face lacks color pigment around the eyes and muzzle.
