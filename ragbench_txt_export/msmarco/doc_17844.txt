One reason to perform these procedure is to gain access to the joint for the purpose of a joint repair or replacement surgery. Cutting into the joint may be necessary to access the surgical field and to make the interior of the joint visible to the physician. It also increases healing time, however. Most arthrotomy incisions are covered by a sterile dressing for a few days to a week after surgery. Orthopedic surgeons must carefully review a patient's x-ray prior to performing an arthrotomy. An arthrotomy will likely be performed by an orthopedic surgeon.
