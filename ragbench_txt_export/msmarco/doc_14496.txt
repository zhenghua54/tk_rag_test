A horse should eat one to two percent of his body weight in roughage every day. Horses who spend much of their time in stalls aren’t doing much grazing, but their natural feeding patterns can be replicated by keeping hay in front of them for most of the day. 1 Two major factors for deciding how much your horse needs to eat are her size and the amount of work she does. 2  Consider the amount of hay or pasture your horse gets: Horses who are grazing on good pasture the majority of the day don’t need much hay, if any.
