Eyeball. The main part of the eye is the eyeball (also known as the globe), which is mostly filled with a jelly-like material called vitreous humor. The eyeball has 3 main layers: the sclera, the uvea, and the retina. Sclera: The sclera is the tough, white covering over most of the outside of the eyeball. Primary intraocular lymphoma (lymphoma of the eye). Lymphoma is a type of cancer that starts in immune system cells called lymphocytes. Most lymphomas start in lymph nodes, which are bean-sized collections of immune system cells scattered throughout the body.
