lamb vs mutton lamb refers to meat from young sheep less than 12 months old which is tender and mild in flavor the meat from a sheep older than one year is called mutton and it has a more intense flavor and somewhat less tender texture and lamb is pretty lean compared to other red meats most of the fat is on the outside not marbled throughout the meat so it s easily trimmed off about 36 percent of the fat in lamb is saturated fat and the rest is mono or polyun
