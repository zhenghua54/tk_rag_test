Individuals and their spouses, if married filing jointly, with earned income are eligible to contribute to a Roth IRA as long as their MAGI meets the following limits: 1  Maximum Roth IRA contribution for 2014: Full contribution if MAGI is less than $114,000 (single) or $181,000 (joint). With a Traditional IRA, you may be able to deduct your contributions on your taxes, which can help lower your tax bill. Your eligibility to deduct is based on your Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) and whether you or your spouse participates in a retirement plan at work.
