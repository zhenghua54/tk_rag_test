Most asymptomatic EIA infected horses will not show any recognizable signs but will test positive on a Coggins test. COGGINS TEST. To insure that an animal is not harboring the virus a simple test is performed, the Coggins test. The Coggins test checks for Equine Infectious Anemia (EIA) antibodies in the horse's blood. Blood samples must be sent to a state approved laboratory. This test is often needed to take your horse to a show and whenever you transport your horse across state lines
