Associated Diseases/Disorders: The pattern and rate of progression of cerebral atrophy depends on the disease involved. Diseases that cause cerebral atrophy include: 1  stroke and traumatic brain injury. 2  Alzheimer’s disease, Pick’s disease, and fronto-temporal dementia. 3  cerebral palsy, in which lesions (damaged areas) may impair motor coordination. 4  Huntington’s disease, and other hereditary diseases that are associated with genetic mutations
