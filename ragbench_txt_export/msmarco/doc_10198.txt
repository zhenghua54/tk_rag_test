Noun. 1. vascular tissue-tissue that conducts water and nutrients through the plant body in higher plants. vascular system-the vessels and tissue that carry or circulate fluids such as blood or lymph or sap through the body of an animal or plant. cambium-a formative one-cell layer of tissue between xylem and phloem in most vascular plants that is responsible for secondary growth. fibrovascular bundle, vascular bundle, vascular strand-a unit strand of the vascular system in stems and leaves of higher plants consisting essentially of xylem and phloem.
