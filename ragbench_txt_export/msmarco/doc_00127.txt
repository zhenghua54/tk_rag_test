Long-term disability insurance usually begins after the short-term disability coverage period ends. It covers a portion of salary in case an employee is out of work for an extended period because of illness. Usually there is no charge to the employee. Short-term disability. When an employee needs to be away from work for an extended period-say, for a nonwork-related disability like maternity, illness, or injury-that employee may have access to a short-term disability (STD) program, which helps provide at least partial income protection.
