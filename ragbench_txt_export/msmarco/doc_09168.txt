Mayonnaise. Mayonnaise is a sauce made with egg yolks, olive or vegetable oil, mustard, and lemon juice or vinegar, and various seasonings. It is one the mother sauces of French cooking and forms the base for a number of other sauces. In French cooking, brown sauces serve as a base for other similar sauces that are used as an accompaniment for meats and vegetables. Brown sauces are made from a brown meat stock and thickened with cornstarch or roux (flour cooked with butter).
