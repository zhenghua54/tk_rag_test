Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. In some people who are exposed to gluten in their diet, an enzyme called tissue transglutaminase changes the gluten into a chemical that causes an immune response, leading to inflammation of the lining of the small intestine. Signs and symptoms of celiac disease may include: 1  diarrhea, 2  bloating, 3  gas (flatulence, farting), 4  swollen ankles (edema), 5  anemia, 6  fatigue, 7  vitamin K deficiency, and. 8  excessive bruising and bleeding.
