Marine iguanas, Amblyrhynchus cristatus, are now listed as Vulnerable (VU B2ac(iv)) on the IUCN Red List of Threatened Species with subpopulations that are either listed as Vulnerable or Endangered due to: The marine iguana Amblyrhynchus cristatus is endemic to the Galapagos Islands, Ecuador. The population occurs as ten subpopulations on separate islands in the range. Total extent of occurrence is less than 5,000 km 2 and area of occupancy is estimated to be less than 500 km 2.
