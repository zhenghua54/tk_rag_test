In a prospective, controlled study comparing open arthrotomy and arthroscopic drainage for the treatment of early septic arthritis of the hip in children, arthroscopic drainage was found to be effective and was associated with fewer days in the hospital than arthrotomy was (3.8 days and 6.4 days, respectively). Daily joint aspirations are usually required until the joint cultures are negative. The knee joint is probably the joint that is most amenable to repeated aspirations.
