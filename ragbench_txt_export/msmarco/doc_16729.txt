This is paracrine-like because neurotransmitters are hormone like – indeed, some can serve also as hormones – but the primary effects of neurotransmitters are only immediately local, i.e., from one neuron (presynaptic) to another (postsynaptic). For more on this topic, see Wikipedia and Google. Contact web master. Paracrine signaling can be loosely viewed as hormone-like signaling though, unlike hormones, the signalling molecules do not enter the blood and therefore have only local immediate effects rather than systemic effects.
