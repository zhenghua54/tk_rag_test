The foil holds moisture in, preventing the butternut squash from drying. Roast the butternut squash until a knife meets no resistance when you insert it in the thickest point of the flesh. Allow the squash to cool after cooking so that you can comfortably touch its skin. Cut and Season. Wash the butternut squash and dry it with a clean towel. Use a sharp knife to cut the butternut squash in half horizontally. Cut the two pieces in half vertically. Remove and discard the seeds from the two lower quarters of the butternut squash with a spoon.
