The Franchise Fee. The franchise fee is just one component of the total costs to start a franchise operation. Pizza Hut's franchise fee is $25,000 for each restaurant, and must be paid when the franchisee signs the franchise agreement, or some other time before the restaurant opens. Service and Other Fees. The new franchise requires working capital and funds for advertising, miscellaneous costs and development fees paid to Pizza Hut. The franchise agreement estimates a requirement of approximately $20,000 to $60,000 for these items, which is paid as incurred. The development fees are not included in this figure -- these amounts vary and must be paid in a lump sum when the franchisee signs a development agreement with Pizza Hut
