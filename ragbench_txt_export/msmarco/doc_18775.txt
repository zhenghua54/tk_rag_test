The First Open Heart Surgery Performed on a Human. The first open heart surgery conducted on a human patient occurred on September 2, 1952. Two surgeons from the University of Minnesota, Dr. Walton Lillehei and Dr. John Lewis, performed the procedure on a fiver-year-old girl with a hole in her heart. The first open heart surgery occurred in the early 1950s after a series of advancements were made in the research of heart problems.
