The adzuki bean (Vigna angularis; from the Japanese アズキ（小豆） (azuki) , sometimes transliterated as azuki or aduki) is an annual vine widely grown throughout East Asia and the Himalayas for its small (approximately 5 mm) bean. The cultivars most familiar in Northeast Asia have a uniform red colour. Adzuki beans are also used to produce amanattō, and as a popular flavour of ice cream either paste, or whole bean such as the 'Cream & Red Bean' product produced by IMEI. On October 20, 2009, Pepsi Japan released an adzuki-flavored Pepsi product.
