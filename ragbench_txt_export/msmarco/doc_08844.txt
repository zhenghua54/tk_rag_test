croup is a condition that causes a swelling of the voice box larynx and windpipe trachea the swelling causes the airway below the vocal cords to become narrow and makes breathing noisy and difficult it is most commonly caused by an infection children are most likely to get croup between 3 months and 5 years of age as they get older it is not as common because the windpipe is larger and swelling is less likely to get in the way of breathing
