Synovectomy is an operation performed to remove partial or all the synovial membrane of a joint. The procedure is either by classic arthrotomy or arthroscopy. Arthroscopic synovectomy has many advantages over open synovectomy: including minimal invasive surgery, short hospital stay, diminution of postoperative joint stiffness, and better complete synovectomy. In an arthroscopic procedure, the excision can be restricted to the areas of involvement in the localized form.
