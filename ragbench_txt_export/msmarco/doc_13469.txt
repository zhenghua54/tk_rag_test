Chiari malformation refers to a deformity of the cerebellum. The cerebellum is the part of the brain that controls coordination and movement execution. It is located at the back and lower portion of the brain. Common symptoms that occur with Chiari malformation include neck pain, dizziness or vertigo, difficulty with vision, coordination problems, and muscle weakness. Symptoms of Chiari malformation may get worse when you cough, sneeze, or stretch your neck.
