Iodine is a trace element found in oceans, the soil and within your body. One major function of the iodine in your body is to convert the food you eat into energy you can use to carry out daily functions. Iodine is also crucial for normal thyroid function. About 70 to 80 percent of the iodine in your body is contained within the thyroid gland in your neck. The rest is spread throughout your body. If your body is too low in iodine, you may develop low thyroid hormone levels, or hypothyroidism
