The Arctic wolf is a sub-species of the grey wolf and lives in the Arctic regions of North America and Greenland. Arctic Wolves (Canis lupus arctos), Montebello, Quebec, Canada.. Thanks to its isolation, the arctic wolf is not threatened by hunting and habitat destruction like its southern relatives. In fact, the arctic wolf is the only sub-species of wolf that is not threatened. Physical Description. Arctic wolves are smaller than grey wolves, They also have smaller ears and shorter muzzles to retain body heat. Size. Length: about 1-1.8m, including tail
