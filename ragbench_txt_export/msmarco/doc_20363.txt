Cardiac Perfusion Scan. Guide. A cardiac perfusion scan measures the amount of blood in your heart muscle at rest and during exercise. It is often done to find out what may be causing symptoms like angina (such as chest pain or pressure). Why It Is Done. A cardiac perfusion scan is done to: 1  Find the cause of unexplained chest pain or pressure. 2  Find the cause of chest pain or pressure brought on by exercise. 3  Check for the location and amount of damage caused by a heart attack. 4  Identify coronary artery disease (CAD) .
