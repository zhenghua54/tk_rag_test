The walrus' scientific name, Odobenus rosmarus, is Latin for tooth-walking sea-horse.. Size relative to a 6-ft (2-m) man: The mustached and long-tusked walrus is most often found near the Arctic Circle, lying on the ice with hundreds of companions. Walruses use their iconic long tusks for a variety of reasons, each of which makes their lives in the Arctic a bit easier. They use them to haul their enormous bodies out of frigid waters, thus their tooth-walking label, and to break breathing holes into ice from below.
