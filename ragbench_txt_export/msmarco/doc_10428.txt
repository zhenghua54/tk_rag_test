Word origin: Iridium comes from the Latin word iris, meaning rainbow. Discovery: British chemist Smithson Tennant discovered iridium in the residue left when crude platinum is dissolved by aqua regia in 1803. Properties of iridium. Iridium is the most corrosive resistant metal known and one of the densest metals. It is a member of the platinum family and is white with a yellowish tinge. Iridium is very hard and brittle, making it difficult to machine, form, or work.[See Periodic Table of the Elements ]. Sources of iridium.
