Allele-A Genetics Definition. An allele is one of two or more versions of a gene. An individual inherits two alleles for each gene, one from each parent. Credit: Darryl Leja, NHGRI. Share. By Regina Bailey. Definition: An allele is an alternative form of a gene (one member of a pair) that is located at a specific position on a specific chromosome. These DNA codings determine distinct traits that can be passed on from parents to offspring through sexual reproduction.
