What CAV is. CAV is the name of a combination of chemotherapy drugs used to treat small cell lung cancer and small cell cancers in other parts of the body. It is made up of the drugs. 1  C = Cyclophosphamide. 2  A = Doxorubicin (also called Adriamycin). 3  V = Vincristine. How you have CAV treatment. You have these drugs into your bloodstream (intravenously). Doxorubicin is a red fluid. Vincristine and cyclophosphamide are clear fluids.
