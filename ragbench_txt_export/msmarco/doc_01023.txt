The company changed names to WorldCom in 1995. The 1998 MCI merger made WorldCom one of the leading players in the global telecom industry. In November 1996, British Telecom offered $21b for the 80% of MCI it did not already own. The proposed deal was a combination of stock and cash. A Short History of WorldCom At the end of 2001, WorldCom was a diversified telecommunications company, providing long-distance and local voice service, data services, and a wide range of internet-related services to corporate and individual clients.
