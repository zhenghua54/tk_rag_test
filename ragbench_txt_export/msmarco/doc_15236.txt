Coeliac disease is caused by a reaction of the gut to gluten. Gluten is part of certain foods-mainly foods made from wheat, barley and rye. Various symptoms can develop including tummy (abdominal) pains, tiredness and weight loss. The cause is a sensitivity to gluten. Gluten occurs in common foods including wheat, barley and rye, and any foods made from these such as bread, pasta and biscuits. Some people with coeliac disease are also sensitive to oats. People with coeliac disease make antibodies against gluten.
