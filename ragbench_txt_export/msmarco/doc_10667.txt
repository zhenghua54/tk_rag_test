Apart from the signs of pericardial effusion, radiological evidence of pulmonary tuberculosis may be present. 1, 7 Pleural effusions are present in about 50% because of tuberculous pleuritis, 7 and a tuberculous granuloma on pleural biopsy can help in a specific diagnosis. Tuberculous pericardial effusion (TPE) is still common in African and Asian countries, 1, 2 but with migrant populations and the spread of AIDS 3 there are now more frequent reports from other parts of the world like Europe and the Western hemisphere. 4, 5 TPE usually presents as a slowly progressive febrile illness.
