A forward contract is an agreement between two parties --- the seller and the buyer --- for the delivery of a certain quality and quantity of a commodity at specified time and for a specified price. Farmers often use forward contracts as hedges against falling prices. If the average price of the commodity has fallen, the contract helps the seller.
