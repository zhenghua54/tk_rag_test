Several different kinds of bacteria can cause food poisoning. Some of the common bacteria include the following: Salmonella and Campylobacter. Normally found in warm-blooded animals, such as poultry and reptiles, also may be present in raw meat, poultry, eggs, or unpasteurized dairy products. Clostridium perfringens. May be present in raw meat, poultry, eggs, or unpasteurized dairy products, as well as in vegetables and crops that come into contact with soil.
