meg·a·pode. n. Any of various large-footed, ground-dwelling birds of the family Megapodiidae, found in Australia, Southeast Asia, and the South Pacific islands, that build mounds or burrows of earth and compost in which to incubate their eggs. Also called mound builder. megapode-large-footed short-winged birds of Australasia; build mounds of decaying vegetation to incubate eggs. mound bird, mound-bird, scrub fowl, mound builder. gallinacean, gallinaceous bird-heavy-bodied largely ground-feeding domestic or game birds.
