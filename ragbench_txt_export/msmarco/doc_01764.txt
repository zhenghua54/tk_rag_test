Per Diem Law & Legal Definition. Per diem is a Latin term meaning per day. It is often used as a basis for payment of daily expenses and/or fees of an employee or an agent. Limitations on hours worked are governed by the individual employer. Per diem rates are usually applicable to temporary employment, such a temporary military service, or expenses such as travel and lodging. The term per diem means daily.. In a business setting, the term has come to mean the daily rates employees use for expenses incurred while traveling on business-related activities. These rates are likely to differ based on whether the employee travels in his or her home area, away from home, or internationally.
