10 Facts About the March Equinox. There are two equinoxes every year – in March and September – when the Sun shines directly on the equator and the length of night and day are nearly equal. The Earth during the equinox. On the equinox the Earth's axis is perpendicular to the Sun's rays. On the equinox, night and day are nearly exactly the same length – 12 hours – all over the world. This is the reason it's called an “equinox”, derived from Latin, meaning equal night. However, in reality equinoxes don't have exactly 12 hours of daylight.
