For the commune in France, see Lect, Jura. In sociolinguistics a variety, also called a lect, is a specific form of a language or language cluster. This may include languages, dialects, registers, styles or other forms of language, as well as a standard variety. Sociolinguists generally recognize the standard variety of a language as one of the dialects of that language.
