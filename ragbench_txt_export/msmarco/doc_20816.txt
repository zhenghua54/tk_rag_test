Best Answer: It means flitted = moved quickly I think it's an archaic word, such as hath was used instead of have, art instead of to be... Here's a list of archaic English words: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_arc... In the time of Alfred Lord Tennyson, -th was often put on the end of verbs, so I am guessing that Tennyson used flitteth to mean flitted or was flitting. Something like She flitteth about like a dove in a grain store.
