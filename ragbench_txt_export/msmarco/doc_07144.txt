An inflammatory disorder typically contracted during the summer and caused by Borrelia burgdorferi, a spirochete transmitted by Ixodes scapularis in the eastern U.S. and I. pacificus in the western U.S., and by various Ixodes species in Europe. (B. valaisiana is another spirochete associated with Lyme disease). Late disseminated disease and chronic lyme disease. Weeks, months, or even years after an untreated tick bite, symptoms can appear in several forms, including: 1  Fatigue, forgetfulness, confusion, mood swings, irritability, numbness.
