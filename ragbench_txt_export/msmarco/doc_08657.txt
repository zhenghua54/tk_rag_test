South Bend was officially incorporated on September 27, 1890. The name of the city comes from its location on the Willapa River. The county seat was relocated from Oysterville to South Bend in 1893. The Pacific County Courthouse is on the National Register of Historic Places. As of the census of 2010, there were 1,637 people, 684 households, and 414 families residing in the city. The population density was 1,004.3 inhabitants per square mile (387.8/km 2). There were 780 housing units at an average density of 478.5 per square mile (184.7/km 2).
