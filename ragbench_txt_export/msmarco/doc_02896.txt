1 Fifth thoracic vertebrae (T5) : The T5 and T4 vertebrae are at the same levels as the sternal angle. 2  The trachea branches into the two main bronchi at the level of T5. 3  The thoracic spinal nerve 5 passes through underneath T5. 4  Sixth thoracic vertebrae (T6) : The thoracic spinal nerve 6 passes through underneath T6. There are 12 thoracic vertebrae (denoted as T1-T12) found in adult humans, and they are situated in between the cervical and lumbar vertebrae, with a general sizing larger than the cervical but smaller than the lumbar vertebrae.
