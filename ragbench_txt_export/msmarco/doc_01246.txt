Cat dander can find its way onto almost everything, from your furniture to the cabinetry and, of course, the floors. Even if the naturally sloughed skin and fur don't trigger allergies in the occupants of your home, dander is a feast for dust mites, so you should remove it from your living quarters. Furry Furniture. Vacuuming dander and fur from upholstered furniture -- at least once a week -- can reduce its accumulation. On the days in between, use a tape or sticky-rubber lint roller designed to remove pet hair.
