Sources of indoor air pollution include: 1  combustion sources such as oil, gas, and wood; 2  tobacco; 3  building materials, including asbestos-containing insulation; 4  furnishings, including flooring, wet or damp carpets, and cabinets or furniture made from certain processed wood products; These indoor air pollution levels are of concern to regulatory agencies because most people spend most of their time indoors. Indoor air pollution can cause immediate health effects, including headaches, dizziness, fatigue, asthma, hypersensitivity to particular substances, and irritation of the ears, nose, and throat.
