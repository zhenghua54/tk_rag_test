It often depends on several variables, including how aggressive your treatment is, your genetic tendencies (for example, some people’s LDL levels shoot down quickly while others’ inch down), and how high your cholesterol levels are to begin with. But one thing is for sure: The higher the LDL cholesterol concentration in your blood, the more plaque build-up you’re likely to have in your arteries, and the more inflamed – and damaged – your artery walls become, leading to progressive vessel blockage and weakness.
