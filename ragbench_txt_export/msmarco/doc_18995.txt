The opposite of halal is haram (forbidden). Food can be forbidden in Islam if it includes: 1  blood. 2  alcohol. 3  meat or any products from a forbidden animal, including pigs and any carnivorous animals or birds of prey. 4  meat or any products of an animal which has not been slaughtered in the correct manner in the name of Allah. So when we are talking about halal foods it means any foods that are allowed to be eaten according to Islamic Sharia law.
