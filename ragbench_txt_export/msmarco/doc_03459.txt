Conjunctivitis. Also called  pink eye , conjunctivitis is inflammation of the clear lining of the surface of the eye, called the conjunctiva. Allergic, bacterial and viral types of pink eye can all result in swollen eyelids, among other symptoms such as watery, red and itchy eyes. Styes. Swollen eyes usually are accompanied by one or more of the following: 1  Eye irritation, such as an itchy or scratchy sensation. 2  Excess tear production, resulting in watering eyes. 3  Obstructed vision (depending on the extent of the swelling). 4  Redness of the eyelid
