Fallopian tubes are a part of the female reproductive system which join the ovaries to the uterus. They are 10-12 centimeters in length, and are present on the either sides of the uterus. Their main function is to transport the egg from the ovary to the uterus. Hydrosalpinx is caused when watery fluid or pus gets filled up in the fallopian tubes resulting in blockage. Sometimes, blockage may also occur if thw tubes get filled with blood. Signs and Symptoms of Blocked Fallopian Tubes. Unfortunately, the symptoms are not very prominent and hence, are not easily identified. Some women do not experience any symptoms at all
