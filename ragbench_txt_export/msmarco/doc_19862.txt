(Answer #1). One effect of the Harlem Renaissance was to help illuminate the discourse concerning race and ethnicity in America. With about a half a century having passed since the end of the Civil War, there was a great deal of challenge in articulating conditions of racial identity in America. The pride in being African and examining what it meant to be African is one of the lasting effects of the Harlem Renaissance. The African-American was shown to be someone who lived on and at the hyphen of American consciousness.
