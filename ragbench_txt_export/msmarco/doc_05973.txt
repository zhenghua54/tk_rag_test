Hydrangeas in Texas. The growing conditions in Texas might not be ideal for the showy hydrangea shrubs that produce massive blue and pink blooms, but with a little bit of care, they can be made to feel at home in most parts of the state. Panicle hydrangea (Hydrangea paniculata), also sometimes called peegee hydrangea, is an Asian native that's hardy in USDA zones 3 to 8, so it's unlikely to do well in the hottest parts of south Texas.
