1 Expect to get fruit 2 to 5 years after planting, if you plant a 1 or 2 year old tree. 2  Remember, all stone fruits bloom very early in the spring. 3  Some years flowers are damaged by freezing temperatures, meaning no fruit that year. 4  But don't worry, you'll probably get fruit next year. Stone fruit trees are easy to grow, provided you accept a few limitations in northern climates. It is important to select a cultivar that is hardy to your USDA hardiness zone. Most stone fruit cultivars are very much at home in zone 5 and higher, but there are a growing number that are proving hardy in colder zones.
