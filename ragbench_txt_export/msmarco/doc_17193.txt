Originally a Chinese appetizer called Hatosi (蝦多士) or shrimp toast, this is a simplified version that makes for a great party canape. Canapé aside from a little cutting and, spreading’they re simple to prepare and are a tasty finger food that goes well with, beer wine or. champagne Spread about a teaspoon of the shrimp mixture on each piece of bread. Make sure to get the shrimp all the way to the edges or the bread will curl while frying. Press a small piece of parsley into each toast for color. Add about 2 inches of oil to a heavy bottomed pot and heat to 350 degrees F.
