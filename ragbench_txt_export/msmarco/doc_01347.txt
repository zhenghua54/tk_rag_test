Usually, judgments are valid for several years before they expire or “lapse.” In some states, a judgment is effective between five to seven years. Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), a judgment can show up on your credit report for at least seven years. It can show up even longer, depending on how much time your state's laws give effect to that judgment. For example, if a judgment was filed against you in Arizona, it is good for five years.
