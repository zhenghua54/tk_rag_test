Anti-NMDA receptor encephalitis, first identified in 2007, is an auto-immune disease that occurs when antibodies turn on the brain and cause it to swell, according to CBS station KYW in Philadelphia. One minute I'd be sobbing, crying hysterically, and the next minute I'd be laughing, Cahalan said to KYW. Susannah Cahalan is now a New York Post reporter who lives in Jersey City, N.J. and has survived anti-NMDA receptor encephalitis with no brain damage.
