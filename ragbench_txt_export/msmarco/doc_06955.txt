In addition to dining on other mantises, praying mantises will eat many other insects like flies, aphids, and beetles. Large mantises have also been known to capture small frogs, mice, and hummingbirds. Mantises use camouflage to ambush their victims and to hide from other predators. The praying mantis is a green or brown insect that uses camouflage and spiked, grasping forearms to capture prey. The praying mantis may have a head shaped like a heart, but it is heartless when it comes to how the female treats her mate.
