Average Income. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the mean annual wage for an electrician was $52,910 as of May 2011. This worked out to $25.44 an hour, and the best paid -- or busiest -- electricians could earn the equivalent of $39.75 an hour or more if they were in the top 10 percent of earners. For instance, many electricians will charge a higher rate for the first hour of work or include additional charges for travel. They may also charge a minimum fee, often the equivalent of one hour of work, for even the simplest and quickest of jobs.
