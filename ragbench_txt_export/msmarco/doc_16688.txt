Noun. 1. sequela-any abnormality following or resulting from a disease or injury or treatment; paralysis is one of the sequelae of poliomyelitis. abnormalcy, abnormality-an abnormal physical condition resulting from defective genes or developmental deficiencies. Translations. n. pl. se·quel·ae (-kwĕl′ē). 1. A pathological condition resulting from a disease. 2. A secondary consequence or result. [Latin. , sequel; see sequel .]
