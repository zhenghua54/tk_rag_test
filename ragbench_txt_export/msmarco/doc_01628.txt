Trans fat is double trouble for your heart health. Trans fat raises your LDL (bad) cholesterol and lowers your HDL (good) (HDL) cholesterol. Find out more about trans fat and how to avoid it. Trans fat is considered by many doctors to be the worst type of fat you can eat. The manufactured form of trans fat, known as partially hydrogenated oil, is found in a variety of food products, including: 1  Baked goods. 2  Snacks. 3  Fried food. 4  Refrigerator dough. 5  Creamer and margarine.
