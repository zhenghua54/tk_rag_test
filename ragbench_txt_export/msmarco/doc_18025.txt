Choosing a Degree. One major difference between an associate's degree program and a bachelor's degree program is the selection of career opportunities available after you graduate. An associate's degree in accounting may qualify you for entry-level positions, such as accounting clerk, bookkeeper or auditing clerk. Associate Vs. Associate's and bachelor's degrees in accounting lead to different career options and have slightly different coursework. Associate's programs typically take two years to complete, while earning a bachelor's degree usually takes four years.
