A sphenopalatine ganglion block is a non-surgical procedure that can be used to diagnose the source of head and face pain. Additionally, it can be used to manage certain types of headache pain, atypical facial pain, neuralgias, and other sympathetic pain conditions. A sphenopalatine ganglion block is a minimally invasive procedure that is performed in an outpatient setting. Physicians have various methods that they can choose from to anesthetize the sphenopalatine ganglion, including the transnasal, transoral, and lateral approach.
