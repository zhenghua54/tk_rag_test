Seafood is a billion dollar industry in Alaska, with more than half of U.S. commercially captured fish caught in the state nicknamed The Last Frontier.. According to Alaska's Department of Labor and Workforce, around 32,200 people fished commercially in Alaska in 2011, averaging 8,064 people per month. Those catching miscellaneous shellfish earned the most, taking in $31,193 annually. Groundfish and sablefish fishermen earned among the least, with annual salaries of slightly less than $21,000.
