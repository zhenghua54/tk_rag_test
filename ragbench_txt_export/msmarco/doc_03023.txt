The Blue-faced parrot finch is easy to cater for and breed in captivity. They will thrive on a good seed mix, the occasional greens, soaked seed and egg food. Blue-faced parrot finches love a good bath, so make sure that you supply clean fresh water daily. They are sometimes found in flocks of 15-30 birds feeding on seeds of grasses, fruits, figs and a variety of live foods including termites and other small insects. Description: Length: 4 to 4 inches (11.5 to 12 cm). The Blue-faced parrot finch is heavy built bird it has a vivid blue face mask.
