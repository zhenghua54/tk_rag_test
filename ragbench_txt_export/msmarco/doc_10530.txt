Shannon and Erne. The Shannon and the Erne. The River Shannon is the longest river in Ireland running over 380km (240 miles) from the Shannon Pot in County Cavan to the sea at Limerick. The river is navigable for most of its length from Lough Allen to Killaloe and provides one of the most peaceful waterways in Europe. There are plenty of places to visit, including some of the best heritage sites in Ireland. The picturesque Kesh River winds through fields to the tiny village of Kesh with a hotel, shops and pubs. The lowest point of navigation on the Erne is the village of Belleek where you can visit the world-famous pottery.
