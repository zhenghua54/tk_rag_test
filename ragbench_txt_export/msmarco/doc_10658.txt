Moreover, nociceptors, like other somatic sensory receptors, arise from cell bodies in dorsal root ganglia (or in the trigeminal ganglion) that send one axonal process to the periphery and the other into the spinal cord or brainstem (see Figure 9.1). Nociceptors. The relatively unspecialized nerve cell endings that initiate the sensation of pain are called nociceptors (noci-is derived from the Latin for “hurt”) (see Figure 9.2).
