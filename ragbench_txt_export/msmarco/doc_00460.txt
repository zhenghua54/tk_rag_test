Causes of vitamin deficiency anemias, also known as megaloblastic anemias, include: Folate deficiency anemia. Folate, also known as vitamin B-9, is a nutrient found mainly in fruits and leafy green vegetables. A diet consistently lacking in these foods can lead to a deficiency. 1 Vitamin C deficiency anemia. 2  Vitamin C deficiency can develop if you don't get enough vitamin C from the foods you eat. 3  Vitamin C deficiency is also possible if something impairs your ability to absorb vitamin C from food. 4  For instance, smoking impairs your body's ability to absorb vitamin C.
