The 2011 Norway attacks were two sequential lone wolf terrorist attacks by Anders Behring Breivik against the government, the civilian population, and a Workers' Youth League (AUF) -run summer camp in Norway on 22 July 2011. The attacks claimed a total of 77 lives. It was the deadliest attack in Norway since World War II, and a survey found that one in four Norwegians knew someone affected by the attacks.
