The name Dungloe or Dunglow is believed to come from the Irish Dun gCloiche. Gcloiche this name came into Common english usage in the later years of the 18th century when the monthly, fair formerly held At gCloiche (dún gcloiche five miles north of the) nascent town was transferred To An. clochan clochán liath It is the main town in The Rosses and the largest in the Donegal Gaeltacht. Dungloe developed as a town in the middle of the 18th Century, and now serves as the administrative and retail centre for the west of Donegal, and in particular the Rosses, with the only mainland secondary school for the area.
