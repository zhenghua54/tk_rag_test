The spleen in humans is a soft organ, and of the size of a fist. It is dark purple in appearance, and 12 cm length, 7 cm height and 4 cm thickness are the average statistics of this organ. The spleen can weigh from 100 to 205 g, depending on the age and health of an adult. For the rest of the life, the organ makes white blood cells to fight infection as a part of the lymphatic system. Factors Which May Interfere with Functions of the Spleen. Enlargement of the spleen is one of the most critical spleen problems.
