The first recorded use of the word buff to describe a colour was in the London Gazette of 1686, describing a uniform to be ...a Red Coat with a Buff-colour'd lining. Buff is the traditional colour of the U.S. Army Quartermaster Corps. The U.S. Army Institute of Heraldry specifies a buff tincture for certain coats of arms. Continental Army uniforms: The Buff and Blue. US Chevron-Quartermaster Sergeant Insignia (1902-1909) Buff on black with blue detail.
