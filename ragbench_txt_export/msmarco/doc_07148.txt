Lyme disease, which is also called Lyme borreliosis, is a vector-borne disease. This term means that it is delivered from one host to another. It is also classified as a zoonosis, which means that it is a disease of animals that can be transmitted to humans under natural conditions. Late disseminated disease and chronic lyme disease. Weeks, months, or even years after an untreated tick bite, symptoms can appear in several forms, including: 1  Fatigue, forgetfulness, confusion, mood swings, irritability, numbness.
