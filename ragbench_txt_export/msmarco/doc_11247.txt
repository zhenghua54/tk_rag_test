Snakes are among the most misunderstood of all animals. As a result, many beneficial and harmless snakes have met untimely deaths at the hands of shovel-wielding humans. Of the nine species currently found in Maine (Table 1), none is capable of inflicting a venomous bite. Food and Feeding Behavior. 1  Snakes are predators and eat many animals thought to be pests, such as mice, voles, snails and slugs. 2  Other prey items include insects, birds and their eggs, fish and frogs.
