Before Dr. Martin Luther King, Junior dreamed of the March on Washington, before he faced down Billy clubs in Birmingham, before he marched on Selma's Edmund Pettus Bridge on Bloody Sunday, he came to Albany to help lead a group of locals and college students and visiting activists in the Albany Movement.
