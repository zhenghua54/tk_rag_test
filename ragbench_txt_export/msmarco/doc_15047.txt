Pleural thickening is the term used to describe the appearance of diffuse (widespread) scarring along the lining (pleura) of the lungs. When this pleural scarring (pleural fibrosis) is limited to one location (focal scarring) it is called pleural plaque. Pleural thickening can occur as a result of any inflammation occurring in the lungs.
