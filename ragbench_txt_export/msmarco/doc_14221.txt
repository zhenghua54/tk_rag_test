Molasses is the dark, sweet, syrupy byproduct made during the extraction of sugars from sugarcane and sugar beets. Molasses can vary in color, sweetness, and nutritional content depending on the variety or how much sugar has been extracted. Molasses Varieties. 1  Light Molasses: This is the syrup left over after the first boiling cycle of sugarcane juice. 2  This molasses is the lightest in color, has the highest sugar content, and the least viscous texture. 3  Dark Molasses: Dark molasses is the byproduct of the second boiling cycle of sugarcane.
