Galapagos marine iguana habitat. This species often lives in colonies where shallow reefs occur with an extensive intertidal zone and a rocky coastline. They are found basking on stretches of low cliffs, about two to five metres above sea level, but may also climb to heights of 80 metres. The Galapagos marine iguana is classified as Vulnerable (VU) on the IUCN Red List and listed on Appendix II of CITES.
