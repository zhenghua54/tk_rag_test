Parkinson's disease is a chronic, degenerative neurological disorder that affects one in 100 people over age 60. While the average age at onset is 60, some people are diagnosed at 40 or younger. 1 Progressive Supranuclear Palsy (PSP). 2  PSP is characterized by early onset of imbalance, frequent falls, rigidity of the trunk, and (eventually) eye-movement problems. 3  Symptoms usually begin after age 50 and progress more rapidly than with Parkinson s disease.
