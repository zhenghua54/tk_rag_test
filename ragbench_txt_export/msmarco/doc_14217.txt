How Molasses Is Made. The most common forms of molasses are made from either sugar cane or sugar beet juice which is boiled down to a syrup. Sugar crystals are extracted from the syrup, and the remaining dark liquid is molasses. Molasses can also be made from sorghum, pomegranate, carob, and dates. Blackstrap Molasses. 1  How it's made: Blackstrap is made from the third and final boiling of the molasses. 2  How it tastes: It is the thickest and darkest in color, and also the least sweet with a pronounced bitter flavor. 3  How to use it: Since it is bitter, only use blackstrap molasses if a recipe specifically calls for it.
