A Petrarchian sonnet is comprised of an octet (8 lines) followed by a sestet (6 lines), all written in iambic pentameter. The rhyme scheme can vary, but it is generally abbaabba in the octet, and either cdecde, cdcdcd, or cdcdee for the sestet. The Spensarian sonnet has an interlocking rhyme scheme of abab,bcbc,cdcd,ee. The Shakespearean sonnet has three quartets and a couplet, and a rhyme scheme of abab,cdcd,efef,gg. The sonnet you will most often see when you study literature in a high school English class is the Shakespearean sonnet.
