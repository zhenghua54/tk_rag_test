Symptoms may depend on what type of leukemia you have, but common symptoms include: 1  A new lump or swollen gland in your neck, under your arm, or in your groin. 2  Frequent nosebleeds, bleeding from the gums or rectum, more frequent bruising, or very heavy menstrual bleeding. 3  Frequent fevers. 4  Night sweats. 5  Bone pain. Leukemia is cancer of the blood cells. It starts in the bone marrow, the soft tissue inside most bones. Bone marrow is where blood cells are made. 1  White blood cells help your body fight infection. 2  Red blood cells carry oxygen to all parts of your body.
