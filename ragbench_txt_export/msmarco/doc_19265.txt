Triglycerides, cholesterol and other essential fatty acids—the scientific term for fats the body can't make on its own—store energy, insulate us and protect our vital organs. An imbalance in any step can result in disease, including heart disease and diabetes. For instance, having too many triglycerides in our bloodstream raises our risk of clogged arteries, which can lead to heart attack and stroke. Fats help the body stockpile certain nutrients as well. The so-called fat-soluble vitamins—A, D, E and K—are stored in the liver and in fatty tissue
