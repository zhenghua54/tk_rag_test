Seeing blood in the toilet, on the outside of your stool, or with wiping after a bowel movement is common. Fortunately, most of the causes of such rectal bleeding are not life-threatening; common causes include hemorrhoids and anal fissures. Bright red blood typically coats the stool or blood may drip into the toilet or stain toilet paper. (See Patient information: Hemorrhoids (Beyond the Basics) .).
