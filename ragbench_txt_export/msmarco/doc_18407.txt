Lean manufacturing is not a head-count reduction system; instead Lean manufacturers understand employees on the shop floor know their work best. Lean manufacturers don’t want employees to work harder, or faster – they want employees to work more efficiently. The difference must then be in how the tools were applied, their appropriateness, but not the tools themselves. 2. Lean is Not Devoid of Technology. The fundamental principle of Lean manufacturing is to design a simple manufacturing system, however, that does not mean eliminating technology.
