Exceedingly nutritious, parsley can be found in your neighboring supermarket. Parsley is the world's most accepted herbs. It gets its name from the Greek word denoting rock celery. It is a vertical, biennial plant with a plump sweet-smelling tap root and dark shimmering leaves which climb from a little stem. Leaves and seeds are the parts that are used as a spice. The smell of the herb is very characteristic, sweet-smelling and spicy due to its volatile oil presence. Common Names. Petroselinum crispum is the biological name of parsley. In the in Indian common languages ie., in Hindi it is called as Ajmood, in Kannada it is called as Achu-Mooda, in Malayalam it is called as Seema malli
