1 Antibiotics will usually clear the infection. 2  An antibiotic is usually prescribed straightaway if a kidney infection is suspected, even before the result of the urine test is known. 3  Some germs (bacteria) are resistant to some antibiotics. Most kidney infections develop as a complication of a bladder infection (cystitis). Germs (bacteria) causing cystitis can sometimes travel up to infect a kidney. The bacteria are usually those which live in the bowel.
