Polaris, or Alpha Ursae Minoris, is the Pole Star or North Star. It is the brightest star in the constellation Ursa Minor. It is almost straight above Earth 's north pole. Because of this, when it is seen from Earth, it looks like it always stays in the same place in the sky. Even though Polaris is the North Star today, this has not always been the case. The place in the sky that the Earth's north pole points at changes slowly over time. This movement is called stellar precession.
