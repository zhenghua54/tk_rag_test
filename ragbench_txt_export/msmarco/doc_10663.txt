Transmission of Viral pericarditis from Person to Person. Viral pericarditis has subtypes that are considered contagious and also has subtypes that are not contagious. Generally the disease can be transmitted by saliva, air, cough, fecal-oral route, surfaces, blood, needles, blood transfusions, sexual contact, mother to fetus, etc., but not ALL subtypes. Viral pericarditis, although infectious, is not a genetic disease. It is not caused by a defective or abnormal gene.
