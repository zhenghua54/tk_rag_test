The meaning of Alexis is 'defender'. It is derived from the word alexein which is of the meaning 'to ward off, to defend'. The feminine use of the name was first popularized by the character Alexis Carrington in the 1980s American soap opera Dynasty, who was portrayed by Joan Collins. Alexis is a very popular baby girl name. The name increased in popularity from the 1960s up to the 1990s. At the peak of its usage in 1998, 1.021% of baby girls were named Alexis.
