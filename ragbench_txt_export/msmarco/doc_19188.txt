Marshes are divided into distinct zones, the high marsh and the low marsh. The difference in elevation between these two areas is usually only a few centimeters, but for the plants that inhabit each of these zones, a few centimeters makes a world of difference. The low marsh floods daily at high tide. Salt marshes are a mosaic of snaking channels called tidal creeks that fill with seawater during high tides and drain during low tides.
