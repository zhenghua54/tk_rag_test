The first typewriter to be commercially successful was invented in 1867 by C. Latham Sholes, Carlos Glidden and Samuel W. Soule in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, USA. The patent was sold to Remington. Remington's first commercial machine appeared in 1873. It was also the first appearance of the QWERTY keyboard. The first typewriter, a invention made by the Austrian carpenter Peter Mitterhofer in 1864, can be seen at the technical museum in Vienna (Austria).
