A full 80 percent of the water used in California goes to agriculture, Gleick points out. “Farmers know how to implement drip irrigation. It’s not a new technology, but they’re learning new ways to do it.” Soil-moisture monitors and smart meters also help reduce waste. “It turns out that with the right feedback delivered in the right way, people cut their water use by 5 percent,” he says. “What we’re trying to offer is a path to market,” he explains. “We have a lot of great technology out there, it just doesn’t make it to market. A lot of our work is reaching out to people who can bring new business models to the sector—that’s almost more important than new technology.”.
