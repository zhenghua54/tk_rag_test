If you're planting dill in rows, keep the rows 2 feet apart. Keep the seeds moist until they germinate then let the soil dry out between waterings. Thin the seedlings to 10 to 12 inches apart when they reach 2 inches tall. Depending on the temperature of the soil, dill seeds should germinate in seven to 21 days. Germinating Indoors. Although you can germinate dill seed indoors and transplant seedlings outdoors when the weather warms, it isn't the ideal method.
