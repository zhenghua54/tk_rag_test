Most people are aware of the health dangers of outdoor air pollution, but may not realize that air pollution in their homes, offices, and schools also can have significant health effects. These indoor air pollution levels are of concern to regulatory agencies because most people spend most of their time indoors. Indoor air pollution can cause immediate health effects, including headaches, dizziness, fatigue, asthma, hypersensitivity to particular substances, and irritation of the ears, nose, and throat.
