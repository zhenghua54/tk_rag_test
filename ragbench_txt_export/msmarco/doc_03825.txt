By September 1985 the US dollar was worth a million pesos bolivianos on the black market. President Paz-Estenssoro announced a free exchange rate for the peso, which was floated on August 29, 1985, resulting in an effective devaluation of 95%. The peso boliviano (ISO 4217 code: BOB), divided into 100 centavos, was the currency of Bolivia from January 1, 1963 until December 31, 1985.
