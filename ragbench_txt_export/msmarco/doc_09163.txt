The epiglottis is a flexible flap at the superior end of the larynx in the throat. It acts as a switch between the larynx and the esophagus to permit air to enter the airway to the lungs and food to pass into the gastrointestinal tract. In its relaxed position, the epiglottis projects into the pharynx, or throat, and rests just posterior to the tongue.
