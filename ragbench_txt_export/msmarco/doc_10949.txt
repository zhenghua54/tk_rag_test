Age 2. This age is the magic number for knowing if a child is on track with speech or is a late talker. Around this period, your growing toddler should be able to: Expand vocabulary. By 24 months, your child should be using about 50 words regularly, such as more, juice, and Grandma. The year between 12 and 24 months is the most exciting year in terms of language development, Dr. Briggs says.
