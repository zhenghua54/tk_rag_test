Most people think of the pericardium as just a double-layered, membranous sac (The Pericardial Cavity) that is around the heart. But, the pericardium is actually TRIPLE layered: the visceral layer, the parietal layer, and the fibrous layer (fibrosa). The visceral pericardium is the inner-most layer of the pericardium, and is also known as the Epicardium as it lays upon the heart. The Pericardial Cavity lies between the visceral pericardium and the parietal pericardium
