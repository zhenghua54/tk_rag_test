Effects. Water weight loss can be dangerous and life threatening because it can cause dehydration. When your body becomes dehydrated, it does not have enough water for basic metabolic processes. In mild cases, your body may take any water it does get and retain it in the tissues, which can lead to bloating. Water weight loss is a temporary reduction in body weight due to the loss of bodily fluids -- generally through sweat, urination or defecation. The body is as much as 60 percent water, which is present in the blood and lymph fluids and absorbed into the tissues
