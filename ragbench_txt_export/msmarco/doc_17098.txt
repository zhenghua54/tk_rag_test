1 Low blood levels of calcium may be caused by low levels of protein (albumin) in the blood, because about half of all calcium in the blood is attached to albumin. 2  For this reason, an ionized calcium level (which is not attached to albumin) and a blood albumin level may also be measured. Low values. Low values of calcium may be caused by: 1  A low level of the blood protein albumin (hypoalbuminemia). 2  Hypoparathyroidism. 3  High levels of phosphate in the blood, which can be caused by kidney failure, laxative use, and other things.
