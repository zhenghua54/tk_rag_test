Incubation begins as soon as the eggs are laid. There are a few erroneous reports of an incubation period of only 9 - 12 days. Since the incubating female must leave frequently to forage for her own food, as well as additional nesting materials, incubation takes longer than expected for such tiny eggs. Realistically, it takes about 14 - 17 days and sometimes up to 23 days of incubation before they hatch. If the weather turns nasty, it extends the incubation period.
