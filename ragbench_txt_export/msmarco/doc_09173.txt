In French cooking, traditional white sauces are one of two types: those made with hot milk added to a white roux (such as Bechamel béchamel sauce Or mornay) sauce or sauces made with hot broth or stock added to a white (roux such As veloute). velouté sauce In French cooking, brown sauces serve as a base for other similar sauces that are used as an accompaniment for meats and vegetables. Brown sauces are made from a brown meat stock and thickened with cornstarch or roux (flour cooked with butter).
