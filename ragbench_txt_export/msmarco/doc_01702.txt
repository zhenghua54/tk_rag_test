Magic Color on Metal: Titanium. As you may know, most of my most personal and ambitious work includes detailed imagery in anodized titanium, a truly marvelous and magical material. Here is an article I wrote that gives some basic information on how that work can happen. This causes the oxide layer to appear colored. The mechanism is the same as for the colors on an oil slick or a soap bubble, but with two important differences. First, it is permanent. Second, the thickness of the layer changes in increments, causing it to produce some colors, and only those colors.
