“Research in Washington (state) shows individuals enrolled in Medicaid experienced 16 percent fewer detentions during the following year than those individuals released without coverage,” said Blandford. “They also found a cost-savings of a $1.06 to the criminal justice system for every $1 spent.”. Matt McKillop a senior research associate from The Pew Charitable Trusts said improper Medicaid payments cost the states $11 billion in 2010. He said the eye-popping amount led Pew to establish its Medicaid anti-fraud and abuse database, www.pewstates.org/Medicaid-fraud.
