Cambridge, Massachusetts: Harvard University Press. The Taj Mahal The crowning jewel of Indo-Islamic architecture, the Taj Mahal is one of the worlds most beautiful and beloved structures. The monument was built in Agra, India, for Mumtaz Mahal, the favorite wife of Mughal emperor Shah Jahan. Construction of the tomb began in 1632 and employed more than 20,000 laborers for 20 years.
