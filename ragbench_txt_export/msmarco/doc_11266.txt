The minting of coins spread rapidly from Lydia to the city states of ancient Greece. Around 600 BC, the island of Aegina produced what are believed to be the first Greek coins. These coins were known as silver drachmae and obols and depicted a turtle on the reverse, the symbol of Aegina. Ancient Coins Collecting is a challenging task, but it's the best way to enjoy and learn history of ancient civilizations. As everyone knows, coins are a form of money or medium of exchange. In addition to coins and paper currency as we now know them, a wide variety of items have been used as money at different times in history, both prior to and after the invention of coins. These items have included, beads, cigarettes, feathers, ivory, cattle, salt, shells, thimbles and wampum
