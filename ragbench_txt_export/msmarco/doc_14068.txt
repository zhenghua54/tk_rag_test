Although the southern civil rights movement first hit the national headlines in the 1950s and 1960s, the struggle for racial equality in America had begun long before. Indeed, resistance to institutionalized white supremacy dates back to the formal establishment of segregation in the late nineteenth century. In her book Praying for Sheetrock Melissa Fay Greene describes how in McIntosh County in the early 1970s, the epic of the civil rights movement was still a fabulous tale about distant places to the black people of McIntosh..
