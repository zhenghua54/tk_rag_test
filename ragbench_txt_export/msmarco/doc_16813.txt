Discovery of Bacteria and Other Achievements. Antony Leeuwenhoek was the first person to see bacteria. Through the late 1670s, he sent comprehensive data and detailed drawings of his sightings of bacteria and algae to the Royal Society in London. Colorized low-temperature electron micrograph of a cluster of E. coli bacteria, Microbe World. Van Leeuwenhoek discovered protozoa - the single-celled organisms and he called them animalcules. He also improved the microscope and laid foundation for microbiology. He is often cited as the first microbiologist to study muscle fibers, bacteria, spermatozoa and blood flow in capillaries
