Earnings Potential. As of 2013, the average annual wage of chiropractors was $78,410, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. The median wage was $65,300 -- meaning that half of chiropractors earned more than this amount and half earned less. According to the BLS, chiropractors tend to earn less early in their careers but increase their earnings as they establish a client base. Top-Paying States. The top-paying state was Alaska, where chiropractors earned an average annual salary of $152,890. Ohio is the second best-paying state for chiropractors, with an annual average salary of $114,260, and New Jersey chiropractors made $101,090 per year.
