Voting in the Jury Room. In order to reach a verdict, jurors need to vote on the major issues of the criminal trial. The federal government and most states require a unanimous vote to convict a defendant. There’s no exact best time the jury has to take a vote during the deliberations. Although jurors can take a vote as soon as they enter the jury room, it’s usually best to first hear all the jurors’ viewpoints and review the evidence and the law. The jurors can take a vote in any manner they want, including written ballots and raising their hands
