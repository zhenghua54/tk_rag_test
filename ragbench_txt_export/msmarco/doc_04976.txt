Since fiber doesn’t have calories by nature of its being indigestible, adding fiber like inulin to processed foods can provide substance and bulk without the calories. Secondly, inulin has a naturally sweet taste; in fact, powdered inulin is marketed as a calorie-free sweetener. Inulin is a type of fiber, meaning it's a plant-based carbohydrate whose bonds cannot be broken by human digestive enzymes. Inulin is also known as a “prebiotic” fiber based on the fact that it's highly fermentable by the friendly bacteria in our guts. Prebiotics are, in essence, like food for “ probiotics ;” or, a source of nutrition for gut bacteria that helps nourish them and enable them to thrive
