1978: In Regents of the University of California v. Bakke, the Supreme Court ruled that the medical school's special admission program setting aside a fixed number of seats for minorities violated Title VI of the 1964 Civil Rights Act. This decision was legislatively reversed with the enactment of the Civil Rights Restoration Act in 1988. 1986: The Court emphasized that lawful affirmative action programs cannot require that incumbent white workers be discharged to make way for minority workers.
