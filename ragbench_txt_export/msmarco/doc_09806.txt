Watches with a Snap-on Back. Work the head of a small screwdriver or the edge of a small, dull knife gently into the groove. Watches with a Snap-on Back. Give the screwdriver or knife a slight twist. This should cause the back plate of the watch to pop off, revealing the battery beneath. Carefully remove the battery and replace it with the new. The back should easily snap back into place. Watches with a Screw-on Back. Push a rubber-tipped opener directly into the center of the back plate of the watch. If you do not have a rubber-tipped opener, you may need to take the watch to a jeweler or purchase a tool kit specifically made for watch repair.
