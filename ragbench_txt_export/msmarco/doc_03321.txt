House Leadership. The Speaker of the House is the only House leadership position mentioned in the Constitution. Over time, as the United States grew and the membership of the U.S. House of Representatives increased, a need for additional leaders developed. Majority Leader. Like the Speaker, the majority leader is elected every two years. The majority party selects the majority leader during meetings before the start of a new Congress. The majority leader is second-in-command to the Speaker of the House.
