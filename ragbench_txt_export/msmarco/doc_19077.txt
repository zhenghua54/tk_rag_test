Common Scheme. Restrictions and Reverters. The existence of a common scheme does not impose restrictions on other lots, but rather identifies those parcels that are entitled to enforce the restrictions. Where, for example, restrictions are imposed on one lot they are not thereby implied as being imposed upon another lot. To the contrary, if there is a common scheme, then the second lot may be able to enforce the restrictions upon the first.
