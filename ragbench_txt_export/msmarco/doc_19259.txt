But our bodies also need a certain amount of fat to function, and we can''t make it from scratch. Triglycerides, cholesterol and other essential fatty acids--the scientific term for fats the body can''t make on its own--store energy, insulate us and protect our vital organs. The so-called fat-soluble vitamins--A, D, E and K--are stored in the liver and in fatty tissues. Using a quantitative and systematic approach to study lipids, researchers have classified lipids into eight main categories.
