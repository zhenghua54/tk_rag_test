Salary Range. According to the BLS, 321,780 people worked as insurance agents nationwide as of May 2011. The average annual wage for these agents was $62,970, or $30.28 an hour. The top 25 percent could make $72,490 or more, with the top 10 percent reaching six-figure salaries of $115,300 or higher. Starting agents at agencies could make a salary in line with the 25th percentile, at $33,050, or the 10th, at $25,450. Insurance carriers ranked second, with 76,380 agents. They could make a salary in line with the 25th percentile, at $37,220, or 10th percentile, at $27,820.
