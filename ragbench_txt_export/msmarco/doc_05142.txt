Mirapex ER (Pramipexole Dihydrochloride) is a dopamine agonist used alone or with other medications to treat Parkinson's disease. It is also used to treat restless legs syndrome (RLS). Common side effects include drowsiness, nausea, dizziness, headache, or dry mouth. The recommended dose of Mirapex ER is one extended-release tablet taken orally once daily, with or without food. Less serious side effects may include: 1  dry mouth, stomach pain, vomiting, constipation; 2  headache, dizziness, spinning sensation; 3  mild drowsiness; 4  swelling in your hands or feet; 5  appetite or weight changes; 6  blurred vision;
