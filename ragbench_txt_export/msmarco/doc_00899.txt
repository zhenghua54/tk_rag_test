re·tic·u·late. adj. 1. Resembling or forming a net or network: reticulate veins of a leaf. 2. Relating to or being an evolutionary process that involves the exchange of genes between organisms of different species, as in the formation of a new species through hybridization. v. (-lāt′) re·tic·u·lat·ed, re·tic·u·lat·ing, re·tic·u·lates. reticulation - (photography) the formation of a network of cracks or wrinkles in a photographic emulsion. photography, picture taking-the act of taking and printing photographs.
