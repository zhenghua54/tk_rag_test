Etymology & Historical Origin-Roxanne. Roxanne is the English and French form of an ancient Greek name Roxana (ρωξάνη) which was ultimately derived from the Bactrian-Persian Roshanak (روشنک) meaning “dawn” (or “bright, radiant one”). Literary Characters of the Baby Name Roxanne. Roxana (The Fortunate Mistress) Roxana Beleau is the title character of Daniel Defoe’s 1724 novel, Roxana: The Fortunate Mistress, purported to be a first person account of her life.
