In dance (particularly ballet), arabesque (. [aʁabɛsk] ; literally, in Arabic fashion) is a body position in which a dancer stands on one leg (the supporting leg) with the other leg (the working leg) turned out and extended behind the body, with both legs held straight. In classical ballet, an arabesque can be executed with the supporting leg en pointe or demi pointe or with foot flat on the floor. The working leg may touch the floor in tendu back (arabesque par terre) or be elevated. Common elevation angles of the raised leg are 45° (a à demi) hauteur and° (90 a à). la hauteur
