An NRE account is a savings or current account held in India that allows the account holder to repatriate funds that come from outside earnings and transfer earnings to India conveniently and securely. Money transferred to an NRE account from any foreign currency is converted to INR. The differences between NRE and NRO accounts are outlined below in the following account features. 1  Repatriation: Repatriation is defined as sending or bringing money back to the foreign country. 2  You can easily repatriate funds from an NRE account including the interest earned in that account.
