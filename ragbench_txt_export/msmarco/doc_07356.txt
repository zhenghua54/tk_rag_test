Aspartame (APM; /ˈaespərteɪm/ ˈæspərteɪm /or/) əˈspɑrteɪm is an, artificial-non saccharide sweetener used as a sugar substitute in some foods and. Beverages in The European, union it is codified As. e951 In the European Union, it is codified as E951. Aspartame is a methyl ester of the aspartic acid / phenylalanine dipeptide. It was first sold under the brand name NutraSweet. It was first synthesized in 1965, and the patent expired in 1992.
